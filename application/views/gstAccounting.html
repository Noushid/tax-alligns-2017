What is SGST, CGST & IGST ?

Goods & Service Tax or GST will be levied on goods and services. It will replace all the various taxes and bring them under one umbrella to make compliance easier. It will replace the major central and state taxes. GST is a consumption based tax, i.e., the state shall collect tax in which goods were consumed.

GST = SGST + CGST + IGST

GST targets ONE NATION ONE TAX by keeping the interest of both central and state government to resource the fund. India is a federal country where both the Centre and the States have the powers to levy and collect taxes. The mechanism of GST and the practical side of the SGST, CGST and IGST are given below.


Levy of the GST is on the basis of supply of goods and services. SGST/CGST/IGST is to be levied by considering the nature of transaction, ie, intra-state or inter-state.


CGST & SGST shall be imposed on Intra-state transaction = Transaction within the state (Eg:- Supply of goods and services from Calicut to Ernakulam)


IGST shall be imposed on Inter-state transaction = Transaction from one state to another state (Eg:- Supply of goods and services from Kerala to Tamil Nadu)

1. CGST & SGST

The central government has the power to levy and collect GST on the supply of goods & services within the same state, known as CGST. At the same time the State governments also have the power to levy and collect GST on the supply of goods & services within the same state, known as SGST. In short, on intra-state transactions, GST rate shall be shared by centre and state equally.

(5%=2.5+2.5<>12%=6+6<>18%=9+9<>28%=14+14)

For e.g.: – A dealer in Calicut sold goods to the consumer in Ernakulam worth Rs. 1,00,000. The GST rate of the item is 5%.
This is a transaction within the same state (Kerala) and termed as intra-state supply. In which the 2.5% tax amount will go to Central Government as CGST and other 2.5% will goes to Kerala Government as SGST.
So ;
1,00,000 x 2.5% = Rs.2,500 to Central Government 1,00,000 x 2.5% = Rs.2,500 to Kerala Government
Total Rs.5,000 (GST @ 5%) levied and collected by Centre and State.

2. IGST

The central government only has the power to levy and collect GST on the supply of goods & services from one state to another state, known as IGST. State government has no roll in IGST.

For e.g.: – A dealer in Kerala sold goods to the consumer in Tamil Nadu worth Rs. 1,00,000. The GST rate of the item is 5%.
This is a transaction between two states Kerala and Tamil Nadu, termed as inter-state supply. In which the full 5% tax amount will go to Central Government as IGST.

So ;
1,00,000 x 5% = Rs.5000 to Central Government. (GST @ 5%) levied and collected by Centre.

3. INPUT TAX CREDIT

For e.g.: –
See the 4 level of transaction of an Item. The GST rate of the item is 5%.

Mohammed Calicut sold goods to Babu Ernakulam worth Rs. 1,00,000.

Babu Ernakulam sold goods to Moorthy Tamil Nadu for Rs.1,30,000

Moorthy Tamil Nadu sold the goods to Final Consumer in Tamil Nadu for Rs.1,50,000

a) Mohammed Calicut sold goods to Babu Ernakulam worth Rs. 1,00,000.

Mr.Babu paid input tax Rs.2500 (2.5%) to Centre as CGST and Rs.2500 (2.5%) to Kerala Govt.
In second transaction Mr.Babu has a tax liability of Rs.6500 as IGST (5% of Rs.130000) for the sales to Moorthy Tamil Nadu. But he has to pay tax (IGST) after set off the credit of CGST Rs.2500 + SGST Rs.2500 (Rs.5000).
The net tax (IGST) he should pay will be Rs.1500 (6500-5000)
In third transaction Mr.Moorthy has a levy of Rs.7000 (Rs.3750 (2.5%) to Centre as CGST + Rs.3750 (2.5%) to Tamil Nadu Govt.)
But he has to pay tax after set off the credit of IGST Rs.6500. When the input in our credit is IGST we should first set off IGST, then CGST and SGST.
CGST payable Rs.3750 – Rs.6500 (IGST Credit) = Rs.2750 (balance in IGST credit)
SGST payable Rs.3750 – Rs.2750 (IGST Credit balance) = Rs.1000 SGST payable
The net tax (SGST) he should pay will be Rs.1000(CGST+SGST Rs.7500-(IGST Rs.6500))

GST ACCOUNTING IN TALLY ERP 9 6

This is about how to get ready with Tally ERP9 6 (latest GST version) for GST accounting
The changes came only in Sales, Purchase, Input GST and Output GST ledgers. Read this article and contact for more understanding

SALES AND PURCHASE LEDGERS

SALES GST @ 5%
PURCHASE GST @ 5%

INPUT GST AND OUTPUT GST LEDGERS

As the GST in India is Dual GST system, we should create 3 input and output ledgers for each rate of GST, ie,

GST LEDGERS FOR INWARD SUPPLY (PURCHASE)

INPUT SGST @ 2.5%
INPUT CGST @ 2.5%
INPUT IGST @ 5%

GST LEDGERS FOR OUTWARD SUPPLY (SALES)

OUTPUT SGST @ 2.5%
OUTPUT CGST @ 2.5%
OUTPUT IGST @ 5%

STEPS TO CREATE ‘SALES LEDGER’

For example create a SALES ledger for an item of 5% in GST

1. Create sales ledger at the rate of 5% under the group of ‘Sales Accounts’

2. Select ‘Applicable’ under Statutory Information

3. Click ‘Yes’ against ‘Set/alter GST details

4. On the Click ‘Yes’ against ‘Set/alter GST details, it will open with a small window – Tax Rate Details

5. Type HSN/SAC Details :- Give description of goods and HSN/SAC codes

6. Select Nature of Transaction as ‘Sales Taxable’ from the given list

7. Give GST rate of item against Integrated Tax

8. When you save the above window, next is to select ‘Goods/Services’

STEPS TO CREATE ‘INPUT/OUTPUT GST LEGDERS’

Under GST Input/Output ledgers are to create with more attention due to dual GST system. One has to create SGST,CGST and IGST ledgers for both Input and Output.

The three ledgers for 5% item are;

INPUT SGST @ 2.5%
INPUT CGST @ 2.5%
INPUT IGST @ 5%

The three ledgers for 5% item are;

OUTPUT SGST @ 2.5%
OUTPUT CGST @ 2.5%
OUTPUT IGST @ 5%

For Eg; See how to create these ledgers from the steps of Input ledger of an item with GST rate 5%.

1. Create ‘Input GST @ 2.5%’ under the group of ‘Duties and Taxes’

2. Select ‘GST’ from the given list

3. Select ‘State Tax’ from the list. Which mean the State Govt. is the authority to levy and collect SGST

4. Give the rate of 2.5% (The rate is 5%, but the half of the rate goes to State as SGST and half rate goes to Central as CGST)

1. SGST & CGST

ചരക്കുനീക്കം സംസ്ഥാനത്തിനകത്താണെങ്കിൽ GST റേറ്റിന്റെ പകുതി സംസ്ഥാന സർക്കാറിനും (SGST) പകുതി കേന്ദ്ര സർക്കാറിനും (CGST) അവകാശപ്പെട്ടതാണ്.
SGST എന്നാൽ State Goodട and Service Tax.
CGST എന്നാൽ Central Goodട and Service Tax.

2. IGST

അതേ സമയം ചരക്കുനീക്കം ഒരു സംസ്ഥാനത്തിനു പുറത്തേക്കാണെങ്കിൽ GST റേറ്റിലുള്ള മുഴുവൻ നികുതിയും കേന്ദ്ര സർക്കാറിനുള്ളതായിരിക്കും (IGST).
IGST എന്നാൽ Integrated Goodട and Service Tax.

അതായത് SGST യും CGST യും സംസ്ഥാനത്തിനകകത്തുള്ള ഇടപാടുകൾക്കും IGST സംസ്ഥാനങ്ങൾ തമ്മിലുള്ള ഇടപാടുകൾക്കും ചുമത്തുന്ന നികുതിയാണെന്ന് മനസ്സിലാക്കുക.

DUAL GST SYSTEM

ഇന്ത്യ ഒരു ഫെഡറൽ സ്റ്റേറ്റ് സ്വഭാവമുള്ള രാജ്യമായതിനാലാണ് Dual GST നടപ്പിലാക്കേണ്ടി വന്നത്. SGST യുടെ കാര്യനിർവഹണം സംസ്ഥാന സർക്കാരുകൾക്കും CGST കേന്ദ്ര സർക്കാരിലും നിക്ഷിപ്തമാണ്

GST COUNCIL

GST നിയന്ത്രിക്കുന്നത് GST കൗൺസിൽ ആണ്. അതിന്റെ ഘടന, അംഗങ്ങൾ, പ്രവർത്തനങ്ങൾ തുടങ്ങിയവ താഴെ തന്നിരിക്കുന്ന വിവരണത്തിൽ നിന്നും മനസ്സിലാക്കുക



GST COUNCIL (Text from http://www.gstcouncil.gov.in); As per Article 279A (1) of the amended Constitution, the GST Council has to be constituted by the President within 60 days of the commencement of Article 279A. The notification for bringing into force Article 279A with effect from 12th September, 2016 was issued on 10th September, 2016.

As per Article 279A of the amended Constitution, the GST Council which will be a joint forum of the Centre and the States, shall consist of the following members: -

Union Finance Minister - Chairperson

The Union Minister of State, in-charge of Revenue of finance - Member

The Minister In-charge of finance or taxation or any other Minister nominated by each State Government - Members



As per Article 279A (4), the Council will make recommendations to the Union and the States on important issues related to GST, like the goods and services that may be subjected or exempted from GST, model GST Laws, principles that govern Place of Supply, threshold limits, GST rates including the floor rates with bands, special rates for raising additional resources during natural calamities/disasters, special provisions for certain States, etc.

The Union Cabinet under the Chairmanship of Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi approved setting up of GST Council on 12th September, 2016 and also setting up its Secretariat as per the following details:



Creation of the GST Council as per Article 279A of the amended Constitution;

Creation of the GST Council Secretariat, with its office at New Delhi;

Appointment of the Secretary (Revenue) as the Ex-officio Secretary to the GST Council;

Inclusion of the Chairperson, Central Board of Excise and Customs (CBEC), as a permanent invitee (non-voting) to all proceedings of the GST Council;

Create one post of Additional Secretary to the GST Council in the GST Council Secretariat (at the level of Additional Secretary to the Government of India), and four posts of Commissioner in the GST Council Secretariat (at the level of Joint Secretary to the Government of India).

The Cabinet also decided to provide for adequate funds for meeting the recurring and non-recurring expenses of the GST Council Secretariat, the entire cost for which shall be borne by the Central Government. The GST Council Secretariat shall be manned by officers taken on deputation from both the Central and State Governments.
